Souffle

Description

A soufflé is a light and airy French dish that showcases the magic of eggs. This classic cheese soufflé is a delightful blend of fluffy egg whites and rich, savory cheese, perfect for impressing guests at a dinner party or enjoying as a luxurious brunch[3]. The word soufflé comes from the French verb "to blow up," which perfectly describes how this dish puffs up in the oven, creating a golden, crisp top with a soft, creamy interior[8].

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Pinch of pepper
  • 3/4 cup milk (1%)
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 cup grated Gruyère cheese (for a cheese soufflé)[3][4]

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C)[4].
  2. Butter a 4-cup soufflé dish and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese[3].
  3. In a medium saucepan, melt butter over low heat. Stir in flour, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture is smooth and bubbly[4].
  4. Gradually whisk in warm milk and bring to a boil, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens[3].
  5. Remove from heat and add egg yolks one at a time, stirring well after each addition[3].
  6. Stir in the grated Gruyère cheese until melted[3].
  7. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites with cream of tartar until stiff peaks form[3][4].
  8. Gently fold one-third of the egg whites into the cheese mixture to lighten it, then carefully fold in the remaining whites[3].
  9. Pour the mixture into the prepared soufflé dish[3].
  10. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes, until puffed and golden brown[3][4].
  11. Serve immediately, as soufflés begin to fall within minutes of removal from the oven[3].
